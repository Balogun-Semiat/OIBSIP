Tribute to Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala

About Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala

Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala is a Nigerian economist and international development expert who has made remarkable contributions to global economic policies. As the first woman and African to serve as the Director-General of the World Trade Organization (WTO), she has been a trailblazer in her field.

Achievements

Throughout her illustrious career, Ngozi has held notable positions such as the Managing Director of the World Bank and twice served as Nigeria’s Finance Minister. She played a pivotal role in securing debt relief for Nigeria, which resulted in a historic $30 billion reduction in the nation’s external debt.

Her leadership extends to serving on boards of prestigious organizations like Twitter, Gavi, and the Global Commission on the Economy and Climate.

Legacy

Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala’s work continues to inspire millions globally. Her dedication to transparency, economic reform, and equitable growth serves as a beacon for aspiring leaders and change-makers.
